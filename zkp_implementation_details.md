# Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) Implementation Architecture

The BlockVote system utilizes two distinct types of Zero-Knowledge Proofs to guarantee end-to-end privacy, eligibility, and computational integrity. This document provides a comprehensive deep-dive into the implementations, the operational flows, and the underlying cryptography.

---

## 1. Groth16 zk-SNARKs (Identity & Eligibility)

The voter-side ZKP is built using **Circom** (v2.0.0) and the **Groth16** proving system. It allows voters to prove they meet the election criteria (e.g., age, valid citizenship) and haven't voted before, without exposing their personal details or their specific vote payload to the public blockchain.

### 1.1 Location & Circuit Context
- **Circuit File:** `circuits/regCheck.circom`
- **Frontend Generator:** `dapp/src/utils/zkpProofGenerator.js`
- **Smart Contract Verifier:** `smart-contract/contracts/Verifier.sol` (autogenerated) & `contract.sol`

### 1.2 Input Signals
The circuit takes a combination of private inputs (kept strictly on the user's device) and public inputs (submitted to the blockchain).

**Private Inputs (Witness):**
- `ic[12]`: The 12-digit Malaysian Identity Card number.
- `voterSecret`: A locally generated random scalar binding the user's session.
- `voterAddress`: The Ethereum wallet address (as a field element).
- `candidateIndex`: The 0-based index of the chosen candidate.

**Public Inputs (10-element array):**
1. `ageThreshold`: Fixed boundary (e.g., 18).
2. `nullifierHash`: `Poseidon(voterSecret, electionId)`. Prevents double voting without exposing the voter's identity.
3. `electionId`: The target election smart contract ID.
4. `voterCommitment`: `Poseidon(voterAddress, voterSecret)`. Binds the generated proof to the caller's wallet.
5. `currentYear`, `currentMonth`, `currentDay`: Clock parameters passed from the blockchain block timestamp.
6. `numCandidates`: Ensures the `candidateIndex` is within the valid range (anti-corruption).
7. `choiceCommitment`: `Poseidon(candidateIndex, voterSecret, electionId)`. Cryptographically locks the encrypted vote payload to the specific candidate without revealing who it is.
8. `payloadHash`: Binds the proof to the outer transaction payload/CID to prevent **Mempool Front-Running (Replay Attacks)**.

### 1.3 Implementation Flow
1. **Compilation Phase:** The `regCheck.circom` code is compiled into a WASM prover and an R1CS constraint system. The `.zkey` artifact holds the trusted setup proving keys. 
2. **Client-Side Generation:** When a user casts a vote, `zkpProofGenerator.js` uses `snarkjs` to compute the Groth16 proof (`pi_a`, `pi_b`, `pi_c`) locally.
3. **On-Chain Verification:** The React app submits the transaction to `Contract.sol`, passing the proof and the 10 public signals. The `verifyVoterWithZKP`/`verifyCandidateWithZKP` functions call the `Groth16Verifier.sol` contract. If the bilinear pairings hold true and the `nullifierHash` hasn't been used, the state updates.

### 1.4 The Mathematics
- **Curve:** alt_bn128 (Ethereum's precompiled ZK curve).
- **Hashing:** Poseidon. Chosen because it is algebraically friendly compared to SHA-256. Calculating Poseidon in a SNARK takes roughly ~250 constraints, whereas SHA-256 requires tens of thousands, making the proof generation extremely fast in the browser.
- **Verification Equation:** $e(A, B) = e(\alpha, \beta) \cdot e\left(\sum_{i=0}^l public_i \cdot \frac{G_i}{\gamma}, \gamma\right) \cdot e(C, \delta)$
Where $e$ is the optimal Ate pairing, $A, B, C$ are the proof elements, and $\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta$ are from the trusted setup.

## 2. Chaum-Pedersen Non-Interactive ZKP (Tally Integrity)

Because BlockVote uses Threshold Paillier encryption for the voting tally, the final decryption requires multiple Trustees to compute a "Partial Decryption" using their private key shares. 

**Vulnerability Prevented:** If a malicious Trustee submits random numbers instead of their real mathematical share, the homomorphic tally combining process would result in permanent garbage data, destroying the election (Denial of Service).

To prevent this, Trustees must submit a **Chaum-Pedersen NIZK of Discrete Logarithm Equality** alongside their share.

### 2.1 Location & Context
- **Logic Module:** `dapp/src/utils/thresholdDecryption.js`
- **Functions:** `generateDecryptionProof()` and `verifyDecryptionProof()`
- **Setup Module:** `smart-contract/phase1-setup.js` (Generates the globally known public verification shares $V_i$)

### 2.2 The Mathematics (Fiat-Shamir Heuristic)
A Trustee holds a secret share $s_i$. 
The public already knows their Verification Share: $V_i = v^{s_i} \pmod{n^2}$.
The encrypted tally is $c$. 
The Trustee calculates their partial decryption: $pd_i = c^{s_i} \pmod{n^2}$.

The Trustee must prove that both $V_i$ and $pd_i$ were exponentiated using the *exact same* secret $s_i$, without revealing $s_i$.

**Step 1: Commitment (Trustee)**
The trustee picks a massive random ephemeral key $w$ (3000+ bits for statistical security).
They compute:
- $A_1 = v^w \pmod{n^2}$
- $A_2 = c^w \pmod{n^2}$

**Step 2: Fiat-Shamir Challenge (Trustee)**
To make the proof Non-Interactive (NIZK), the trustee hashes the transcript to generate a deterministic challenge $e$:
$e = \text{SHA256}(v \parallel V_i \parallel c \parallel pd_i \parallel A_1 \parallel A_2)$

**Step 3: Response (Trustee)**
The trustee calculates the boundary response $z$, evaluated over the integers (not modulo $n$, because the order of $v$ modulo $n^2$ is obscured):
$z = w + (e \cdot s_i)$

The final exported proof payload is: `{ pd_i, e, z }`.

### 2.3 Verification Flow
When the Organizer collects the shares to finalize the election, the system runs `verifyDecryptionProof()` for every trustee before accepting their data into the combiner.

1. The organizer extracts the submitted $e$, $z$, and $pd_i$, along with the known public variables ($v, V_i, c$).
2. The organizer algebraically recomputes what $A_1$ and $A_2$ must have been:
   - $A_1' = (v^z \cdot V_i^{-e}) \pmod{n^2}$
   - $A_2' = (c^z \cdot pd_i^{-e}) \pmod{n^2}$
3. The organizer re-hashes the new transcript:
   - $e_{\text{calc}} = \text{SHA256}(v \parallel V_i \parallel c \parallel pd_i \parallel A_1' \parallel A_2')$

**Conclusion:** 
If $e_{\text{calc}} \equiv e$, it is cryptographically impossible for the Trustee to have faked the partial decryption. The share is safely aggregated. If it fails, the system logs "Malicious Partial Decryption detected" and drops the corrupted data.
